Adaptive Dual MA Trend Ribbon Community “Larsson-Style” Presets

A dual-moving-average “trend ribbon” for TradingView that allows traders to experiment with SMMA / EMA / SMA / WMA pairs and optional ATR-based neutral zones. The indicator is free to use on TradingView; the script is protected.

Open on TradingView

How Traders Recreate This Without the Indicator

At its core, the different “Larsson-style” trend ribbons are based on a very simple idea: plotting two smoothed moving averages and observing how they interact over time.

If you want to recreate a similar structure manually in TradingView, you can do the following:

  1. Add a Smoothed Moving Average (SMMA) to your chart.
  2. Set its length to 15 and leave the remaining inputs at their defaults.
  3. Add a second Smoothed Moving Average with the length set to 29.
  4. When the faster SMMA (15) moves above the slower SMMA (29), the trend bias is bullish.
  5. When the faster SMMA (15) moves below the slower SMMA (29), the trend bias is bearish.

Because moving averages are lagging by nature, this approach tends to work best on higher timeframes (daily and above) and during clear directional trends. It performs poorly in sideways or choppy markets, which is why many traders combine it with volatility filters, market structure, or other forms of confirmation.

The indicator provided here packages this concept into a cleaner visual ribbon, adds optional ATR-based neutral zones, and makes it easier to experiment with different moving-average types and lengths.

Overview

Many traders enjoy experimenting with dual-moving-average “trend ribbon” structures that resemble the visual style of popular tools such as the Larsson Line. While the internal logic of closed-source indicators is not publicly available, the trading community often explores MA combinations like SMMA 15/29 or EMA 30/60 with ATR filters to study trend transitions and visualize momentum shifts.

This script does not replicate, reverse-engineer, or replace any closed-source indicator. Instead, it provides a flexible framework that lets traders build their own trend-ribbon configuration inspired by similar visual concepts while maintaining transparency around inputs and behavior. Because the calculations rely only on standard moving-average and ATR methods, traders can experiment freely with community-discussed presets without relying on proprietary tools.

What the Script Does

  • Plots Fast and Slow moving averages using SMMA (RMA), EMA, SMA, or WMA.
  • Colors the ribbon to show directional bias:
    • Bullish when Fast MA > Slow MA
    • Bearish when Fast MA < Slow MA
    • Neutral when the difference is small (optional ATR filter)
  • Supports ATR-based neutral zone filtering to highlight choppy conditions.
  • Supports optional bar coloring by trend state.
  • Works on any market and timeframe.
  • Free public indicator; code is protected on TradingView.

Why This Script Is Useful

Traditional moving averages identify trend direction but often lack context during uncertain or low-momentum conditions. This ribbon adds structure in a few ways:

  1. Allowing multiple smoothing techniques (SMMA/EMA/SMA/WMA)
  2. Highlighting clearer trend transitions
  3. Identifying low-confidence periods using ATR
  4. Providing a visually intuitive ribbon instead of single-line signals

Suitable for swing traders, trend followers, breakout traders, and anyone who wants cleaner structure-based contextualization.

How to Use

  1. Choose your preferred MA type
  2. Adjust Fast/Slow lengths to match your timeframe
  3. Enable ATR Neutral Zone to reduce false flips
  4. Optionally enable bar coloring for additional clarity
  5. Combine with structure, volume, or price action for decision-making

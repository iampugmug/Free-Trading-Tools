Adaptive Dual MA Trend Ribbon Community “Larsson-Style” Presets

Many traders enjoy experimenting with dual-moving-average “trend ribbon” structures that resemble the visual style of popular tools such as the Larsson Line. This indicator provides a clean, configurable ribbon implementation for TradingView. The indicator is free to use; the script is protected.

How Traders Recreate This Without the Indicator

At its core, the different “Larsson-style” trend ribbons are based on a very simple idea: plotting two smoothed moving averages and observing how they interact.

  1. Add a Smoothed Moving Average (SMMA) to your chart.
  2. Set its length to 15 and leave the remaining inputs at defaults.
  3. Add a second SMMA with length set to 29.
  4. If the 15 SMMA is above the 29 SMMA, bias is bullish; if below, bias is bearish.

Because moving averages lag, this tends to work best on daily and higher timeframes and during clear trends. It performs poorly in choppy ranges, so many traders combine it with structure, volatility filters, and other confirmation.

What the Script Does

  • Plots fast/slow moving averages using SMMA (RMA), EMA, SMA, or WMA.
  • Colors the ribbon:
    • Bullish when Fast MA > Slow MA
    • Bearish when Fast MA < Slow MA
    • Neutral when the difference is small (optional ATR filter)
  • Optional ATR-based neutral zone filtering to reduce chop.
  • Optional bar coloring by trend state.
  • Works on any market/timeframe.

Popular Community Presets

  • SMMA 15 / 29 (classic smoother structure)
  • EMA 30 / 60 (more reactive)

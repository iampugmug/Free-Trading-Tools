Adaptive Dual MA Trend Ribbon Community “Larsson-Style” Presets

Dual-moving-average “trend ribbon” for TradingView that lets you experiment with SMMA/EMA/SMA/WMA pairs and ATR-based neutral zones, inspired by community Larsson-style ribbons. The indicator is free to use; the code itself is published as protected on TradingView.

Open on TradingView

Overview

Many traders enjoy experimenting with dual-moving-average “trend ribbon” structures that resemble the visual style of popular tools such as the Larsson Line. While the internal logic of closed-source indicators is not publicly available, the trading community often explores MA combinations like SMMA 15/29 or EMA 30/60 with ATR filters to study trend transitions and visualize momentum shifts.

This script does not replicate, reverse-engineer, or replace any closed-source indicator. Instead, it provides a flexible framework that lets traders build their own trend-ribbon configuration inspired by similar visual concepts while maintaining full transparency on inputs and behavior. Because the calculations rely only on standard moving-average and ATR methods, you can experiment freely with community-discussed presets without relying on proprietary tools.

What the Script Does

  • Plots Fast and Slow moving averages using SMMA (RMA), EMA, SMA, or WMA.
  • Colors the ribbon to show directional bias:
    • Bullish when Fast MA > Slow MA
    • Bearish when Fast MA < Slow MA
    • Neutral when the difference is small (optional ATR filter)
  • Supports ATR-based neutral zone filtering to highlight choppy conditions.
  • Supports optional bar coloring by trend state.
  • Works on any market and timeframe.
  • Free public indicator; code is protected on TradingView.

Why This Script Is Useful

Traditional moving averages identify trend direction but often lack context during uncertain or low-momentum conditions. This ribbon adds structure in a few ways:

  1. Allows multiple smoothing techniques (SMMA/EMA/SMA/WMA).
  2. Highlights clearer transitions between bullish, bearish, and neutral phases.
  3. Uses ATR to flag low-confidence, sideways periods.
  4. Provides a visually intuitive band instead of a single line.

It is well-suited for swing traders, trend followers, and breakout traders who want a clean context filter to sit alongside their existing entry and risk rules.

Popular MA Combinations

  • SMMA 15 / 29 – smoother structural trend flow.
  • EMA 30 / 60 – more responsive momentum shifts.
  • EMA 10 / 21 – intraday rhythm.
  • EMA 50 / 100 – higher-timeframe structure.

These examples are commonly discussed in the community, but this script does not recreate or imitate any closed-source or commercial indicator.

How to Use

  1. Choose your preferred MA type and lengths based on your timeframe.
  2. Enable the ATR Neutral Zone if you want fewer noisy flips.
  3. Optionally color bars by trend for faster visual reads.
  4. Combine with structure (support/resistance), volume, and your own entry triggers.

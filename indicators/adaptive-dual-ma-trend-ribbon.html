How Traders Recreate This Without the Indicator

At its core, the different “Larsson-style” trend ribbons are based on a very simple idea: plotting two smoothed moving averages and observing how they interact over time.

If you want to recreate a similar structure manually in TradingView—without using this indicator—you can do the following:

  1. Add a Smoothed Moving Average (SMMA) to your chart.
  2. Set its length to 15 and leave the remaining inputs at their defaults.
  3. Add a second Smoothed Moving Average with the length set to 29.
  4. When the faster SMMA (15) moves above the slower SMMA (29), the trend bias is bullish.
  5. When the faster SMMA (15) moves below the slower SMMA (29), the trend bias is bearish.

Because moving averages are lagging by nature, this approach tends to work best on higher timeframes (daily and above) and during clear directional trends. It performs poorly in sideways or choppy markets, which is why many traders combine it with other indicators and technical analysis for confluence.

You don’t need to use 15 and 29 — feel free to experiment with your own settings. This indicator is provided as a free tool for exploration and convenience.

Overview

Many traders enjoy experimenting with dual-moving-average “trend ribbon” structures that resemble the visual style of popular tools such as the Larsson Line. While the internal logic of closed-source indicators is not publicly available, the trading community often explores MA combinations like SMMA 15/29 or EMA 30/60 with ATR filters to study trend transitions and visualize momentum shifts.

This script does not replicate, reverse-engineer, or replace any closed-source indicator. Instead, it provides a flexible framework that lets traders build their own trend-ribbon configuration inspired by the visual concept of similar tools while maintaining complete transparency around inputs and behavior.

Because all calculations use standard, well-known moving average methods, traders who enjoy experimenting with community-discussed MA presets can do so freely, without relying on proprietary tools. (The indicator is free to use; the script is protected.)

What the Script Does

  • Plots Fast and Slow moving averages using the method of your choice: SMMA (RMA), EMA, SMA, or WMA.
  • Colors the ribbon to show directional bias:
    • Bullish when Fast MA > Slow MA
    • Bearish when Fast MA < Slow MA
    • Neutral when the difference is small (optional ATR filter)
  • Supports ATR-based neutral zone filtering.
  • Supports optional bar coloring tied to trend state.
  • Works on all markets and timeframes.
  • Free to use on TradingView (script protected).

Why This Script Is Useful

Traditional moving averages identify trend direction but lack context during uncertain or low-momentum conditions. This script adds value by:

  1. Allowing multiple smoothing techniques (SMMA/EMA/SMA/WMA)
  2. Highlighting clearer trend transitions
  3. Identifying low-confidence periods using ATR
  4. Providing a visually intuitive ribbon instead of single-line signals

Suitable for swing traders, trend followers, breakout traders, and anyone who wants cleaner structure-based contextualization.
